**Phishing Awareness Training**
Overview

This project is a Phishing Awareness Training Presentation designed to educate users about phishing attacks, social engineering techniques, and cybersecurity best practices. The training material aims to help individuals recognize and avoid common phishing scams that target personal and organizational information.

The presentation is intended for students, employees, and anyone interested in improving their cybersecurity awareness.

**Objectives**

The training focuses on the following objectives:

Understanding what phishing is and how it works.
Identifying common characteristics of phishing emails.
Recognizing fake websites and fraudulent login pages.
Understanding social engineering tactics used by cybercriminals.
Learning practical steps to prevent phishing attacks.
Testing knowledge through interactive quiz questions.
Topics Covered

1. Introduction to Phishing
Definition of phishing
Types of phishing attacks
Why phishing remains a major cybersecurity threat

3. Recognizing Phishing Emails
Suspicious sender addresses
Urgent or threatening language
Unexpected attachments
Fake links and malicious URLs
Grammar and spelling mistakes

5. Identifying Fake Websites
Checking website URLs
HTTPS and SSL certificates
Domain spoofing techniques
Warning signs of fraudulent websites

7. Social Engineering Tactics
Impersonation attacks
Authority-based scams
Fear and urgency tactics
Pretexting and baiting
Business Email Compromise (BEC)

9. Best Practices for Protection
Verifying sender identities
Avoiding suspicious links
Using multi-factor authentication (MFA)
Keeping software updated
Reporting phishing attempts

11. Real-World Examples
Examples of phishing campaigns
Case studies of successful attacks
Lessons learned from cybersecurity incidents

13. Interactive Quiz

The presentation includes quiz questions to help reinforce key concepts and assess understanding.

Sample Questions:

What is the primary goal of a phishing attack?
Which of the following is a sign of a phishing email?
What should you do if you receive a suspicious email?
Why is MFA important for security?
